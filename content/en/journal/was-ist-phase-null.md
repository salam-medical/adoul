Anyone looking to build, convert or repurpose a building often thinks immediately of floor plans, building permits and costs. Understandable. But the decisive questions come earlier:

Is the project feasible at all? Is the site suitable? Which use makes sense — economically, legally, in urban terms? What will the building cost, not just to build, but across its entire life cycle?

These questions belong to **Phase Zero** — a preparatory project phase that isn't explicitly anchored in the classic HOAI structure, but that in practice determines whether a project succeeds or fails.

## What does Phase Zero cover?

Phase Zero isn't a clearly defined catalogue of services, but an umbrella term for all the structured activities that take place before Service Phase 1 (basic evaluation). These typically include:

**Site and context analysis** — What does the zoning plan allow? What's the infrastructure like? Which neighbouring uses are relevant? What does the existing building or site reveal about its potential?

**Defining objectives** — What does the client really want to achieve — and is that goal realistic? Aspiration and feasibility often diverge widely at the outset. Phase Zero brings the two into alignment.

**Feasibility assessment** — Is the project approvable? Which use scenarios are conceivable? What are the economic parameters?

**Involving relevant stakeholders** — Who needs to be brought in early — authorities, neighbours, future users? Early involvement saves time and conflict later on.

**Life-cycle thinking** — A building isn't finished once it's built. Operation, maintenance and possible future repurposing should be considered from the very start.

## Why Phase Zero matters so much

Mistakes made — or avoided — in Phase Zero affect every subsequent planning phase. A wrongly defined use, an overlooked planning-law constraint, or an unrealistic cost estimate at the outset can cause a project to fail years later.

The reverse is also true: those who take Phase Zero seriously make better decisions — faster, with less friction, and with a higher-quality result.

## Phase Zero isn't an add-on — it's the beginning

In public perception, architecture begins with the first design. In reality, it begins with the right question. Phase Zero is where these questions are asked, structured and answered — before money is spent on planning that can't be recovered later.
