With its closed-off kitchen and strictly separated rooms, this apartment from 1972 no longer met today's expectations of open, contemporary living. The aim of the conversion was to rethink the existing structure and create a living atmosphere that combines generosity, calm and everyday practicality.

By restructuring the floor plan, the kitchen, dining area and living room were merged into one open living space. The result is a flowing area that connects cooking, dining and living while offering room for both togetherness and retreat. An integrated library and reading corner adds a quiet, personal place to the space.

The private area was also reorganised: the bedroom is now arranged separately and directly connected to its own bathroom. Natural materials, coordinated colour tones and the green kitchen fronts give the project its calm, characterful identity.
